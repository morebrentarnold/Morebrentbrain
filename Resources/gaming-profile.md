# Gaming Profile & Preferences

## Core Gaming Taste
- Prefers strong narrative-driven single-player experiences
- Values immersion, cinematic presentation, and polished world-building
- Likes action combat when it feels impactful/responsive
- Enjoys progression systems and RPG mechanics, but only when they support pacing/story
- Strongly prefers games that respect player time and avoid unnecessary grind

## Favorite / Highly Enjoyed Games
- The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt
- Mass Effect 2 (one of all-time favorites)
- Ghost of Tsushima
- The Last of Us Part I (really really liked)
- Marvel's Spider-Man Remastered
- Batman: Arkham Asylum

## Mixed / Didn't Fully Click
- **Cyberpunk 2077** — felt like should have liked it more than actually did
- **Kingdom Come: Deliverance II** — similar reaction; didn't get excited despite seeming aligned on paper
- **God of War** — felt repetitive
- **Baldur's Gate 3** — surprisingly could not get into it
- **Uncharted 4** — likes story/graphics/mechanics but worried about repetitive climbing/sneaking
- **Expedition 33** — disliked timing-based combat enough to uninstall

## Likes in Game Design
- Strong character writing / party dynamics
- Momentum and pacing over simulation/realism
- Combat that feels skillful but not punishing for punishment's sake
- Clear progression and upgrades
- Premium/high-production-value presentation
- Distinct set pieces / memorable moments
- Forward narrative momentum

## Dislikes / Friction Points
- Repetitive traversal / filler mechanics
- Overly punishing or obtuse systems
- Slow starts / delayed payoff
- Excessive micromanagement / inventory/admin burden
- Turn-based combat has mixed appeal
- Timing/parry-heavy mechanics can reduce enjoyment if over-centralized
- Systems that feel "designed to be realistic" over "designed to be fun"

## Platform / Play Habits
- MacBook Pro M5
- Steam / Steam Deck
- Values suspend/resume friendliness and pick-up-and-play flexibility
- Typically plays in 20-30 minute bursts (two kids)

## Recommendation Heuristics
When suggesting games:
- Bias toward narrative-heavy action RPGs / action adventures
- Favor polished AA/AAA experiences over experimental/indie unless exceptionally aligned
- Avoid recommending purely because of genre similarity — must match pacing/feel/tone
- Prioritize games with strong early hooks
- Be cautious recommending highly systems-heavy CRPGs or sim-heavy RPGs

## Previously Considered / Discussed Titles
- Days Gone
- Ninja Gaiden Black

- Final Fantasy VII Remake

## Overall Summary
Refined AAA action-adventure/RPG taste. Best recommendations should feel like:
**"Prestige single-player blockbuster with strong pacing and emotional/narrative payoff."**
