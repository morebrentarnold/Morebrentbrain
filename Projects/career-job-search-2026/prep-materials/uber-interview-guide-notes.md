# Uber Interview Guide — Notes

Source: https://jobs.uber.com/en/uber-interview-guide/

## Uber Values

1. **Go get it** — *Bring the mindset of a champion*
   Hustle, embrace the grind, overcome adversity, play to win for the people we serve.
   → **Your angle**: Shipping Facebook Simplification across multiple halves under ambiguity. Staying hands-on even at a senior level.

2. **Trip obsessed** — *Make magic in the marketplace*
   Judgment to make difficult trade-offs, blend algorithms with human ingenuity, create simplicity from complexity.
   → **Your angle**: Your Rider POV (simplicity over noise). Design systems as a way to create simplicity at scale.

3. **Build with heart** — *We care*
   Products profoundly affect lives. Care drives us to perfect our craft.
   → **Your angle**: "I stay close to craft because I care about the quality of what ships." Your selective hands-on involvement.

4. **Stand for safety** — *Safety never stops*
   Embed safety into everything. Always be better for the communities we serve.

5. **See the forest and the trees** — *Know the details that matter*
   Big picture AND details. Small improvements compound into enormous impact.
   → **Your angle**: This is your management philosophy in one line. System-level thinking + inflection point involvement.

6. **One Uber** — *Bet on something bigger*
   Work together for what's best for Uber, not the individual. Disagree and commit. Celebrate progress together.
   → **Your angle**: Org consolidation answer — unifying two teams around a shared north star, not individual legacy.

7. **Great minds don't think alike** — *Diversity makes us stronger*
   Diversity of ideas, identity, background, experience, education.

8. **Do the right thing** — *Period*

## Uber Design Principles
Excerpted from **"77 Things"** — Uber Design's internal primer.

1. **Act responsibly** — Design with awareness of impact
   → Your angle: "Build with heart" value + safety-conscious design thinking

2. **Put design at the center** — Design has a seat at the table, not just execution
   → Your angle: Ask Bud directly — "How does design influence product strategy at Uber?" This principle signals it should.

3. **Say 'we' not 'me'** — Collaborative, team-first mindset
   → Your angle: Org consolidation answer — One Uber, shared north star, not individual legacy teams

**Note:** "77 Things" is worth looking up if you can find it — it's Uber Design's full philosophy doc.
