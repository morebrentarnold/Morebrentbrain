# Uber Recruiter Call Prep - Jennifer

## Call Details
- **Scheduled**: [Book here: https://calendar.app.google/5G4bsNzYNt2bHNff7]
- **Type**: Introductory/screening call
- **Duration**: Likely 30 minutes
- **Recruiter**: Jennifer (Uber Talent Acquisition)

## Call Objectives

### What Jennifer Wants
1. Confirm your interest and availability
2. Assess basic qualifications fit
3. Gauge communication skills
4. Screen for red flags
5. Set expectations for process

### What You Want
1. Make a strong first impression
2. Demonstrate genuine interest in Uber/role
3. Understand the interview process and timeline
4. Learn about team structure and priorities
5. Advance to hiring manager round

## Your 2-Minute Intro (Prepare & Practice!)

> "Thanks so much for reaching out, Jennifer. I'm really excited about this opportunity.
>
> I'm a product design leader with [X] years of experience, including [Y] years leading and scaling design teams. Most recently, I [brief current/recent role summary].
>
> What drew me to this role is [1-2 specific things about Uber/Rider]. I've spent my career [key theme from your background], and the opportunity to lead design for Uber's Rider experience—which touches millions of people daily—is exactly the kind of high-impact work I'm looking for.
>
> I'm particularly excited about [mention 0-1 product work / strategic design / team building / something from the job description that aligns with your strengths]."

**Keep it under 90 seconds. Practice saying it out loud.**

## Questions Jennifer Will Likely Ask

### 1. "Tell me about yourself / Walk me through your background"
→ Use your 2-minute intro above

### 2. "Why Uber? Why this role?"
**Your Answer:**
- Uber is the leader in mobility/marketplace design at scale
- Rider app is the core product—high visibility and impact
- Excited by the complexity of multi-sided marketplace
- [Personal connection: do you use Uber? Care about transportation? Cities?]
- Design leadership opportunity to build/mentor a team

### 3. "What's your experience leading design teams?"
**Your Answer:**
- "I've led teams of [X] designers across [contexts]"
- "My approach focuses on [your philosophy: quality + velocity / user-centered / data-informed / etc.]"
- Brief example: "For instance, I [specific accomplishment with team impact]"

### 4. "Do you have experience building 0-1 products?"
**Your Answer (CRITICAL - they emphasized this!):**
- "Yes—I led [product name] from concept to launch"
- Impact: "[user metric / business outcome]"
- What made it successful: "[your role in vision, research, execution]"
- *If you don't have 0-1:* Focus on major redesigns, new features, or greenfield initiatives

### 5. "What's your salary expectation?"
**Your Answer:**
- They've posted the range: $241k-$268k base
- "I saw the posted range of $241-268k. That's in line with my expectations, and I'm more focused on the overall package and opportunity. Can you share more about the total comp structure?"
- OR: "I'm open to discussing compensation once we determine mutual fit. Can you share the full compensation structure for this role?"

### 6. "When could you start if we moved forward?"
**Your Answer:**
- Be realistic about notice period
- "I'd need to give [2 weeks / 1 month] notice, so approximately [timeframe]"
- "I'm committed to a smooth transition for my current team, but I'm very motivated to move quickly."

### 7. "Are you interviewing anywhere else?"
**Your Answer (be honest but strategic):**
- "I'm exploring a few opportunities, but Uber is definitely a top choice because [specific reason]"
- OR: "I'm early in my search and being selective. This role stood out because [specific reason]"

### 8. "What questions do you have for me?"
→ See questions section below

## Your Questions for Jennifer

### Process & Logistics (Pick 2-3)
1. **"What does the interview process look like from here?"**
   - How many rounds?
   - Who will I meet?
   - Timeline?

2. **"What's the typical timeline for making a decision?"**

3. **"What are the key things you're evaluating for in this role?"**

### Role/Team (Pick 2-3)
4. **"Can you tell me more about the team I'd be leading?"**
   - How many designers?
   - What are they working on?
   - Current team strengths/gaps?

5. **"What are the biggest priorities for the Rider team right now?"**

6. **"How does design work with product and engineering at Uber?"**
   - Influence on roadmap?
   - Decision-making process?

7. **"What makes someone successful in this role at Uber?"**
   - Beyond the job description, what separates good from great?

### Company (Pick 1)
8. **"What do you enjoy most about recruiting for Uber's design org?"**

9. **"How would you describe Uber's design culture?"**

## Red Flags to Watch For
- Vague answers about team structure or priorities
- Unrealistic timeline expectations
- Lack of clarity on interview process
- Defensive responses to questions
- High turnover mentions

- [ ] Download Uber Rider app and use it

- [ ] Look up Jennifer on LinkedIn
- [ ] Read 2-3 recent articles about Uber

## During the Call
- **Smile** (they can hear it in your voice)
- **Listen actively** - let Jennifer finish before responding
- **Take notes** - capture key details about process/team
- **Be conversational** - not interrogative
- **Show enthusiasm** - genuine energy matters
- **Ask for next steps** before ending

## After the Call
- Send thank you email within 24 hours
- Note key takeaways in the Uber company doc
- Update timeline and next steps
- Prepare for hiring manager round if advanced
