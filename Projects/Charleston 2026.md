		- **Morning (10:00–11:00)** — Start easy at **Babas on Cannon**, a neighborhood cafe that locals swear by. Coffee, a light bite, and good energy to ease into the day.
		
		- **Midday Oysters (11:00–12:00)** — Walk down to **167 Raw** on lower King Street. Walk-in only, so arriving right at 11 when they open means you'll get seated fast. Their raw oysters, lobster rolls, and ceviche are staples — and the scallop po' boy is worth trying. [Post and Courier](https://www.postandcourier.com/food/charleston-dining-guide-restaurants-drinks-food/article_ec5fdd8d-cb40-43f7-b195-4ade394f3ed7.html) Everything here works perfectly for your diet.
		
		- **Art & Culture (12:30–2:15)** — The **Gibbes Museum of Art** is Charleston's premier American art museum, compact enough to enjoy without fatigue. Then stroll a block south to **Gallery Row** on Broad Street and the French Quarter galleries. There are two distinct gallery districts — one on Broad Street and one in the French Quarter just a block north. [Charming Inns](https://charminginns.com/guide/art-galleries/) Drop into The George Gallery, Principle Gallery, or Reinert Fine Art at your own pace.
		  
		- **Scenic Breather (2:15–3:00)** — Quick walk past **Rainbow Row** (the iconic pastel houses on East Bay) and into **Waterfront Park** for harbor views and the Pineapple Fountain. Sit on a bench, take it in.
		  
		- **Cocktail Hour (3:00–4:15)** — **The Gin Joint** opens at 3 PM on Fridays, steps from the waterfront. Try their bartender's choice — pick two flavor words and let them build you something custom. [Post and Courier](https://www.postandcourier.com/mycharleston/food_scene/10-of-charlestons-best-cocktail-bars/article_a74c978c-dc9e-4c21-8425-a1b8c5dca8fa.html) One of the best cocktail bars in the South.
		  
		- **Wine Interlude (4:30–5:15)** — Head up to **Graft Wine Shop & Bar** on upper King. Constantly changing selection from small producers, with happy hour on weeknights from 4 to 6 PM. [Substack](https://americanweekender.substack.com/p/charleston-south-carolina-bars) Mellow vibe, perfect for a glass before the evening picks up.
		  
		- **One bonus:** The Charleston Festival (house and garden tours) runs March 18 through April 11, 2026 [Go South! Charleston](https://gosouthcharleston.com/things-to-do/charleston-in-april.html), so there may be some special tours or events happening that Friday too. Worth checking closer to the date.

**Cocktail bars for after dinner**

- **The Annex at Last Saint** — A speakeasy-style back room with only about 20 seats, no menu — bartenders custom-make drinks based on your spirit and flavor preferences. [Toastontheeastcoast](https://www.toastontheeastcoast.com/blog/6-best-charleston-sc-cocktail-bars) Book a reservation on Resy. This one's right up your alley.

- **The Gin Joint** — Widely considered Charleston's best cocktail bar, with a "bartender's choice" option where you pick two flavor words and they build something for you. [American Distilling Institute](https://distilling.com/distillermagazine/top-10-charleston-bars/)

- **Dalila's** — A Caribbean-inspired cocktail bar on Spring Street, petite and intimate, perfect for a couple. Happy hour runs 5–7 PM with great deals. [Wayward Blog](https://www.waywardblog.com/best-bars-charleston-south-carolina/)

- **Graft** — Part wine shop, part wine bar with a constantly changing selection. Great for a more mellow vibe with happy hour on weeknights from 4–6 PM. [Substack](https://americanweekender.substack.com/p/charleston-south-carolina-bars)

- **Citrus Club** at The Dewberry Hotel — rooftop tropical cocktails with the highest public viewpoint in the city. [Charleston Guru](https://charlestonguru.com/guide/best-places-for-cocktails-in-charleston/) Great sunset spot.
