# Solo Trip — Late March / Early April

**Goal:** 2 nights, solo, active, decompression from family/work burnout.
**Timing:** Late March – early April 2026
**Home base:** Rowayton, CT — drive-only, ~4 hr max

## Top Options

### Snowboarding (Vermont — 3-4 hrs from Rowayton)
⚠️ **Likely off the table** — Left Achilles is only 3 months post-op. Snowboard boots lock the ankle; a fall or edge catch could re-injure. Confirm with surgeon/PT before considering.
- **Killington, VT** (~3.5 hrs) — Best late-season resort in the East. Revisit when Achilles is cleared.
- **Stowe, Sugarbush, Jay Peak** — All strong options for a future season.

### Achilles-Safe Options
- **Canyon Ranch, Lenox MA** (~2.5 hrs) — Best fit given bilateral recovery. Pool swimming, upper body strength, guided meditation, proper food. Designed for exactly this kind of reset. Pricey but worth it.
- **Newport, RI — food + flat cycling** (~2 hrs) — Ocean Drive loop is flat tarmac, low Achilles load if cycling is cleared by PT. Great solo restaurant scene, easy walking on flat ground.
- **Whitewater rafting — Deerfield River, MA** (~2.5 hrs) — ⚠️ Check with doctor: seated the whole time but getting in/out of raft on riverbank could be risky at 3 months post-op.
- **Fly fishing — Catskills, NY** (~2 hrs) — ⚠️ Wading rules this out. Could do a float trip (drift boat) where you're seated — ask guide outfitters about that option.

## Top Pick
**Canyon Ranch, Lenox MA** — Given bilateral Achilles recovery (left only 3 months post-op), this is the most realistic active trip. Swimming, upper body work, recovery programming. A genuine reset without re-injury risk.
